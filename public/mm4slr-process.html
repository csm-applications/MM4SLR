Mature Process for Systematic Literature Reviews

1. Planning

Define the review motivation and goals
  • Provide a basis for the paper using actual research
  • Ensure study contributes value to research or practice
  • Ensure that report include a clear statement of research aims and objectives
  • Provide an adequate description of the context in which the research was conducted
Define protocol questions
  • Define and document the systematic review in a detailed protocol
  • Select and apply a well-established guideline for SLR process
  • Revise research questions during protocol development as understanding increases
  • Perform a pilot test in the SLR protocol
  • Involve all review team members in developing the protocol
  • Validate your protocol externally to reduce rework
  • Allocate adequate time for protocol development and anticipate revisions
  • Establish a validation process separate from protocol piloting
Develop a search plan
  • Use a formal and well-experimented search strategy to identify all relevant publications
  • Adopt reliable and efficient digital libraries or search engines
  • Refine the search strategy iteratively until acceptable
  • Inspect search strategies with experts to reduce iterations and effort
  • Balance high sensitivity and precision to retrieve relevant studies
  • Consider limitations of online databases when designing and executing search strategies
Prepare the environment
  • Form an SLR team of three people to balance bias avoidance and coordination
  • Develop efficient and clear communication strategy with the team
  • Provide support and guidance to novice researchers
  • Familiarize reviewers with field terminology and process
  • Adopt support resources adequate for researcherâ€™s experience
  • Adopt tools to manage the full SLR process and monitor progress

2. Conduction

Select and evaluate the studies
  • Identify and mitigate the search & selection bias
  • Identify and mitigate publication bias
  • Use collaborative tools/resources to detect and resolve inter-rater reliability problems
  • Use tools to appraise quality of experiments in primary studies
  • Develop strategies to design and apply quality assessment of primary studies
  • Keep detailed record of all decisions made during the review process
Extract and analyze the data
  • Conduct a comprehensive and rigorous process of data extraction and analysis
  • Cross-check results from data extraction with team members
  • Identify and mitigate data extraction bias
  • Select appropriate formal methods to perform data analysis
  • Use text analysis tools to support specific stages
  • Adopt strategies to automatize SLR tasks
  • Use structured knowledge representations for complex tasks
Quality management and control
  • Record all relevant information during the review process
  • Provide documentation and external materials to track SLR procedures
  • Use tools agnostic of electronic databases to manage references
  • Adopt strategies to minimize wasting efforts and optimize tasks

3. Results Report

Results synthesis
  • Decodify aggregated data into textual descriptions
  • Ensure the study presents results clearly
  • Discuss limitations and threats to validity
  • Present sufficient data to support findings
  • Report conclusions and implications appropriately
  • Choose appropriate reporting format
  • Use formal guidelines and strategies to report SLR
  • Publish a full report separately from a journal overview
Knowledge sharing and dissemination
  • Make raw data available
  • Share protocols within the community
  • Take as baseline good SLR examples and protocols
  • Adopt strategies to share knowledge inside/outside research groups
  • Provide documentation and materials to ensure replicability
